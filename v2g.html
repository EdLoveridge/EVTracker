04 — BESS

How does V2G compare to grid-scale batteries?

The UK is rapidly building utility-scale Battery Energy Storage Systems (BESS). But a V2G-enabled EV fleet represents a far larger distributed storage resource — and one that grows automatically as EVs reach mass market.

UK BESS capacity today
12.9 GWh
Installed grid-scale battery storage as of 2025 — predominantly co-located with solar and wind assets.
Predicted BESS by 2030
~50 GWh
National Grid ESO forecast for grid-scale storage needed to support 2030 clean energy targets.
EV fleet storage (5M EVs, 2030)
300 GWh
5 million EVs × 60 kWh battery. Even at 30% usable V2G capacity that's 90 GWh — nearly 2× the 2030 BESS target.
EV vs 2030 BESS target
6×
The potential EV storage pool is six times larger than the predicted 2030 grid-scale BESS target.
Unlike fixed BESS assets, EV batteries come pre-financed by vehicle owners — the grid gets storage for free, and drivers get paid. V2G effectively turns every driveway into a micro power station. Even if only 10% of a 5M EV fleet participated, that's 30 GWh of dispatchable storage — more than twice the entire UK grid-scale BESS capacity today, and enough to power roughly 1.5 million homes for a full day.
