UK Grid · Vehicle-to-Grid · Interactive Simulator

What could EVs do
for the UK grid?

Vehicle-to-Grid (V2G) technology lets EV batteries discharge electricity back to the grid during peak demand periods. Adjust the sliders below to see how a V2G-enabled fleet could flatten the daily demand curve and reduce peak load.

03 — Household

One home, one EV

Zoom in from the national grid to a single household. The 7.2 kW V2G charger is larger than the typical evening peak demand (~4.5 kW) — so the EV battery can cover most or all of a home's evening load entirely. The "usable V2G capacity" slider above controls how much of the 60 kWh battery is available here too.

04 — BESS

Comparison to BESS

Current Battery Energy Storage System (BESS) capacity is 12.9GWh

By 2030, this is predicted to be ~50GWh

If the UK has 5 million EVs on the road by 2030 (~ government target), the total capacity will be ~300GWh

This capacity is 6x larger than predicted BESS, and even if only partially unlocked it could provide significant grid support
